Goal 9: Circular Dignity

Overview & Photos

1. Executive Summary

"Neki Ki Diwar" presents a transformative opportunity to convert textile waste into a powerful social and environmental resource. Ahmedabad, as a major textile hub, produces significant quantities of waste that ends up in landfills. This community-driven, circular economy solution seeks to divert usable garments from landfills, support a circular economy through sorting and refurbishment, and create women-centric livelihood opportunities.

2. Project Objectives

  • Establish a structured textile collection ecosystem across Ahmedabad.
  • Divert usable garments from landfills through community participation.
  • Promote garment reuse and advance circular economy principles.
  • Create dignified livelihood opportunities for women through SHG engagement.
  • Develop a scalable, replicable, and financially sustainable model.

3. Implementation Methodology

  • Phase 1: Collection - Sourcing from residential societies, schools, corporates, and community drives via mobile vehicles.
  • Phase 2: Sorting - Categorizing by Gender, Type, and Condition (Reusable/Refurbish/Recycle).
  • Phase 3: Refurbishment - Process includes Washing, Drying, Repair/Stitching, Ironing, and Quality Checks.
  • Phase 4: Packaging - Tagging with Category, Size, Gender, Unique ID, and Minimal Sale Price.
  • Phase 5: Distribution - Channels include Mobile Retail Units, Community Sales Camps, and NGO Referrals.
  • Phase 6: Recycling - Upcycling non-usable garments into cleaning cloths, bags, mats, and crafts.

4. Women Empowerment & Strategy

The initiative engages 50+ women through Self Help Groups (SHGs). They are trained in sorting, quality assessment, tailoring, upcycling, packaging, and inventory management. This provides them with sustainable income, entrepreneurship skills, and digital literacy. The project involves a dedicated Warehouse Facility with strict inventory management using daily stock registers and digital MIS tools.

5. SDG Alignment

This initiative directly aligns with the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs):

  • SDG 1: No Poverty - Generating sustainable livelihoods for vulnerable communities.
  • SDG 5: Gender Equality - Empowering women through skill development and SHG engagement.
  • SDG 8: Decent Work and Economic Growth - Creating entrepreneurship and employment opportunities.
  • SDG 12: Responsible Consumption and Production - Advancing a circular textile economy.
  • SDG 13: Climate Action - Reducing carbon footprint by diverting waste from landfills.

6. Expected Outcomes

  • 5,000+ units of Textile Waste Reduced
  • 3,000+ Garments Redistributed
  • 1,500+ Upcycled Products Created
  • 2,000+ Families Benefited
  • 50+ Women Empowered

Beyond numbers, this initiative will build a replicable community-owned model of a circular textile economy that can be scaled across Gujarat and nationally.
