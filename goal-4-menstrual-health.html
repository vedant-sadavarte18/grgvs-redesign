Goal 4: Women Empowerment

2. Swasthya Saheli

Introduction: Education is a key factor in achieving sustainable development goals, and it is crucial to ensure that adolescent girls are not left behind. In India, many girls drop out of school due to menstruation-related issues, with 23% dropping out when they reach puberty. The aim of this project is to address menstrual hygiene management in government schools for adolescent girls in order to increase school attendance and reduce the dropout ratio.

Project Description: Targeting girls in classes 6, 7, and 8 in government schools in Gujarat, India, the program focuses on increasing awareness about menstruation, hygiene practices, and eliminating related stigma and taboos. The program also increases the availability and accessibility of sanitary napkins in schools. By actively engaging with adolescent girls, teachers, and mothers, misinformation is eliminated, empowering girls to manage their menstruation with dignity and continue their education.

Impact & Conclusion: By increasing awareness, eliminating stigma, and providing resources, the project aims to reduce absenteeism, increase school attendance, and create a supportive learning environment. This directly contributes to achieving sustainable development goals in India by empowering girls and their communities.

Swasthya Saheli – Menstrual Hygiene Awareness Program

Overview: Swasthya Saheli is a focused CSR initiative designed for adolescent girls (aged 9–13 years) in Rural & Urban Government Schools across the Ahmedabad District. With a target of 1,000 beneficiaries, the program adopts a preventive, school-based approach through interactive awareness sessions led by trained female facilitators.

Key Interventions & Activities:

  • Interactive Awareness: Using culturally appropriate Gujarati IEC materials, sessions cover topics like puberty, menstruation, hygiene, nutrition, and myth-busting.
  • Product Support: Distribution of menstrual hygiene kits (sanitary napkins and hygiene essentials) accompanied by demonstrations on correct usage and safe disposal.
  • Teacher Engagement: Orientation of female teachers for continuous follow-up guidance and support within the schools.

Impact & Expected Results: The project aims to improve menstrual health knowledge, increase confidence and emotional preparedness before menarche, promote hygienic practices, reduce social stigma, and ultimately curb school absenteeism (which is at 20-30% during menstruation in rural areas). By addressing these challenges early, the program empowers girls to continue their education with dignity.

Alignment with SDGs:

  • SDG 3: Good Health and Well-being
  • SDG 4: Quality Education
  • SDG 5: Gender Equality
  • SDG 6: Clean Water and Sanitation
